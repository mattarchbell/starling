This section is about the members of the group

Chelsea Penlington

I am a recent graduate of the Environmental & Resource Science program at Trent University. I have worked on two land cover classification projects with Lower Trent Conservation and Cataraqui Region Conservation Authority as a student. I have also done volunteer work at Trent University with environmental modeling and spatial interpolation in the soil science field. My main areas of interest are conservation, precision agriculture and more generally how GIS can be applied in these fields to solve environmental problems. I am looking forward to furthering developing my skills within these fields through this collaborative project with the CRCA.

Matthew Archbell

I previously studied Wildlife/Conservation Biology at U of Guelph. I recently worked with Peterborough District MNRF on invasive water soldier mapping in the Trent Severn and fish sampling and agricultural drainage classification in southern Ontario for DFO as a student. My main interests lie in applying GIS in a conservation and natural resource management setting. Remote sensing courses at Fleming have really piqued my interest in large scale landscape classification and machine learning applications.

Tayler Nichol

My background is in environmental geoscience and archaeology at the University of Western Ontario, which allowed me to explore a variety of disciplines, including hydrology and paleoenvironmental analysis. I am now learning GIS web development techniques at Fleming College. I have previously worked on hazard mapping research at UWO, and environmental planning projects as an intern with Hydro One. One of my primary research interests is the use of remote sensing and GIS technologies for natural carbon sequestration and landscape restoration. I am looking forward to using my experience and skills to develop watershed land cover classification workflows with the CVCA.
